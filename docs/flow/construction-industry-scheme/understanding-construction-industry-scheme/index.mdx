## What is CIS?

CIS requires:

- Contractors deduct tax from payments
- Deductions sent to HMRC
- Rates: 0%, 20%, or 30%
- Applies to construction work
- Requires proper documentation

## Who Needs CIS?

### Contractors

- Pay subcontractors
- Must deduct tax
- Submit returns to HMRC
- Maintain records

### Subcontractors

- Receive payments
- Get deductions made
- Claim deductions back
- Submit returns

## CIS Deduction Rates

### Gross Payment (0%)

- No deduction
- Verified subcontractor
- Verified with HMRC
- Requires verification

### Standard Rate (20%)

- 20% deduction
- Most common rate
- Standard subcontractors
- Verified status

### Higher Rate (30%)

- 30% deduction
- Unverified status
- Default for new
- Until verification

## How CIS Works in Flow

### Client Setup

- Enable CIS for client
- Set payment status
- Add UTR number
- Configure NINO

### Invoice Creation

- Enable CIS toggle
- Rate auto-populates
- Categorize line items
- CIS calculates automatically

### CIS Breakdown

- Labor value shown
- Materials value shown
- CIS deduction amount
- Net payment due

## CIS Calculations

### Labor Items

- CIS deduction applies
- Rate based on status
- Calculated automatically
- Shown in breakdown

### Materials

- No CIS deduction
- Excluded from calculation
- Shown separately
- Included in total

### Other Items

- Typically no deduction
- Depends on nature
- Check HMRC guidance
- Verify with accountant

## Compliance

### Record Keeping

- Maintain CIS records
- Keep invoices
- Document deductions
- Submit returns

### HMRC Requirements

- Monthly returns
- Payment deadlines
- Verification checks
- Proper documentation
